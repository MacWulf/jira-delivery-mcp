# Interview Planning

Build the interview plan before deeper questioning.

## Required topics

- domain and problem
- stakeholder presence
- current state
- target state and scope
- constraints and risks
- derived acceptance direction

## Planning rules

- mark each topic as `known`, `must ask`, or `optional`
- prefer the smallest question set that still allows a reliable backlog handoff
- ask one question per message
- avoid compound questions
- ask about stakeholder presence before stakeholder role mapping
- once stakeholder presence is answered, mark it as resolved and do not schedule it again unless later input contradicts it
- do not reserve a separate acceptance block; derive acceptance later

## Stop conditions

Discovery can stop when:
- the domain is clear enough
- the problem and desired change are stable
- stakeholder model is identified
- current and target state are clear at a useful level
- scope can be split into in-scope and out-of-scope
- remaining uncertainty can be captured as open questions

Default ceiling:
- at most 3 major rounds
- summarize after 5 to 10 questions or at topic completion
